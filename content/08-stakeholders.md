# Stakeholder Engagement

## Stakeholder Landscape

Successful delivery at government scale demands deliberate, inclusive, and transparent stakeholder engagement. The program maintains a living **Stakeholder Register** with over 400 identified stakeholders across four categories.

---

## Stakeholder Categories

### Category 1: Executive Sponsors
Senior government leaders who champion the program and have final approval authority.

| Stakeholder | Role | Engagement Level |
|---|---|---|
| Federal CIO | Program Executive Sponsor | Quarterly briefings, FMSC chair |
| Agency Deputy Secretaries (×12) | Agency Sponsors | Monthly executive dashboards |
| OMB Deputy Director for Management | Oversight | Quarterly progress reports |
| Congressional Budget Office Liaison | Appropriations | Bi-annual briefings |

### Category 2: Agency Stakeholders
IT and business leaders within each participating agency.

| Stakeholder | Role | Engagement Level |
|---|---|---|
| Agency CIOs (×12) | PCB Members | Monthly PCB meetings |
| Agency CISOs (×12) | Security Sign-off | Bi-weekly security reviews |
| Agency Product Owners (×30+) | PI Planning | Every 12 weeks |
| Agency Business SMEs | User Research | As required per workstream |
| End Users (Federal Employees) | UAT, Feedback | Sprint reviews, UAT cycles |

### Category 3: Oversight & Compliance
Bodies responsible for program accountability and regulatory compliance.

| Stakeholder | Role | Engagement Level |
|---|---|---|
| GAO (Government Accountability Office) | Independent Oversight | Annual briefings |
| OIG (Office of Inspector General) | Audit | As requested |
| DHS CISA | Cybersecurity Oversight | Quarterly security posture reviews |
| NIST | Standards Guidance | Advisory |

### Category 4: Delivery Partners
Vendors, contractors, and technical partners contributing to program delivery.

| Stakeholder | Role | Engagement Level |
|---|---|---|
| Prime Contractor | Program Delivery | Daily (embedded in ARTs) |
| Cloud Service Providers (AWS, Microsoft) | Infrastructure | Monthly service reviews |
| Independent IV&V Contractor | Quality Assurance | Quarterly assessments |
| Specialty Subcontractors | Domain expertise | PI-level reviews |

## Engagement Strategy

### Communication Channels

| Channel | Audience | Frequency | Owner |
|---|---|---|---|
| Executive Dashboard (PowerBI) | FMSC, PCB | Monthly (live) | PMO |
| Program Newsletter | All stakeholders | Monthly | Comms Lead |
| Town Halls | All program staff | Quarterly | Program Director |
| Agency Briefing Packs | Agency CIOs | Monthly | PMO |
| Sprint Reviews (public) | Agency SMEs | Bi-weekly | RTE |
| Risk & Issue Updates | PCB | Monthly | Risk Manager |
| Social / Intranet Updates | Federal workforce | Weekly | Comms Lead |

### Feedback Mechanisms
- **Agency feedback portal** — asynchronous submission of concerns or suggestions
- **Post-PI surveys** — quantitative satisfaction and effectiveness ratings from all ART participants
- **Stakeholder pulse checks** — bi-annual qualitative interviews with senior stakeholders
- **Citizen satisfaction surveys** — NPS tracking for all launched digital services

## Change Management (OCM)

### Change Impact Assessment
Before each major release or organizational change, a **Change Impact Assessment (CIA)** is conducted:
- Who is affected, and how?
- What behaviors/processes must change?
- What training and support is required?
- What is the resistance risk, and who are the change champions?

### Agency Readiness Framework
Each agency is assessed for readiness across 5 dimensions before system go-live:

| Dimension | Assessment Method |
|---|---|
| Technical Readiness | Infrastructure checklist, connectivity test |
| Operational Readiness | Runbook review, ops team training completion |
| User Readiness | Training completion rates, UAT pass rates |
| Process Readiness | SOP updates validated by agency SMEs |
| Leadership Readiness | Executive sponsor confirmation and comms sent |

### Training Program
- **Role-based training paths** for system administrators, end users, and executives
- **Self-paced e-learning** modules hosted on the program LMS
- **Instructor-led workshops** for complex system transitions
- **Quick reference guides** and video walkthroughs for every major release
- **Super User / Ambassador network** — 2–3 trained change agents per agency

## Diversity, Equity & Inclusion (DEI) Commitments

The program is committed to ensuring that modernization benefits are equitably distributed:

- **Accessibility-first design** — all digital services meet WCAG 2.1 AA before launch
- **Plain language** — all citizen-facing content written at Grade 8 reading level or lower
- **Multilingual support** — top 10 languages supported across citizen portals
- **Rural and low-connectivity access** — offline/low-bandwidth modes for critical services
- **Inclusive research** — user research panels include underrepresented and vulnerable populations
